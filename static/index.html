What do we see in 1/10th of a second?

Project ID: 22806

You are invited to take part in this study. Please read this Explanatory Statement in full before deciding whether or not to participate in this research. What does the research involve?

The study will investigate how quickly people recognize what kind of information graphic they are looking at and its main components. This will inform our understanding of how data graphics are perceived and may also inform the design of "glanceable" visualizations such as those on a smartwatch which are designed to be understandable at a glance. In order to help us with this evaluation, you will be asked to complete a user study by using a computer online and you can access the experiment from your home or wherever it is convenient. The time the participants take to complete each task and their answers will be recorded with coded identification, i.e., Participant 1, Participant 2, and so on, in order to link the result of each experiment. No user name or contact details is recorded in any electronic data during the experiment. You will go through the following steps:

  1. Be shown a copy of the Consent Form and asked to agree if you wish to proceed.
  2. Answer a few questions concerning your experience with the graphics in the experiment and demographic questions about age, gender, and education.
  3. Be trained with the necessary background to understand these graphics and answer the questions in the study.
  4. Be shown a sequence of these graphics for short periods of time (1/10th of a second or less). After being shown each graphic you will be asked to identify the kind of graphic and answer a multiple-choice question about it.

We expect that you will need about 20-30 minutes to complete this study.

What kind of graphs will be displayed for this research?

The kinds of graphics that will be used in the study are line graphs, scatter plots, bar charts, pie charts, and network diagrams. You should be familiar with these to take part in the study.

Consenting to participate in the project and withdrawing from the research

Participation in this study is entirely voluntary, and you are under no obligation to complete the experiment. You will be sent this explanatory statement and consent form through Email after you contact us. You will also be emailed with an explanatory statement and consent form before the experiment starts. You will be asked to fill it and click on agree to provide your consent in the form if you wish to proceed.

The user study will be conducted online. All the digital data is collected using coded surveys, e.g., participant 1, participant 2, and so on. No user name or contact details are recorded in any survey or visualization task completion during the experiment, except the consent form which contains the name of the participant. An online demographic survey is used to collect background information of the participants (age, gender, degree, graph skill level, visualization skill level). During the experiment, participants are presented with visual representation images and asked to take on visualization tasks made of multiple-choice questions using a computer. At the end of each experiment, an online survey is used to collect their experience of the experiment and which visual presentation they preferred.

Possible benefits and risks to participants

The study is low-risk. However, because the participants will be presented with all the static images and visualization tasks from a computer monitor, there is a small chance that the participants may experience fatigue. In that case, we remind the participants that he/she can take a break or stop the session.

By participating in the user study, you can help us understand the comprehension of the graphs when displayed for a quick glance.
